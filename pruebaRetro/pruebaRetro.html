Apple Computer Museum

Apple II

Released in 1977, this was the world's first succesful mass-produces computer. It was mainly designed by Steve Wozniak.

Macintosh

Released in 1984, this was the first commercially successful personal computer to use a graphical user interface.

Apple I

Released in 1976, it was the first computer to combine a microprocesor with a keyboard and a monitor. Hand made by Steve Wozniak.

The LISA

Released in 1983, it was one of the first computers to use a GUI. Unfortunately, it was a failure due to its high price and the release of the Macintosh. Controversially named after Steve Jobs' daughter.

iMac

Released in 1998, this was the first major redesign of the Macintosh computer in over a decade.

iPod

Released in 2001, this was the first portable digital music player with a large storage capacity and easy-to-use interface.
